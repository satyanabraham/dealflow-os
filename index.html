Pre-money valuation$8,000,000

Clause analysis — VC weight + founder impact
Liquidation (1× non-participating): Founder-friendly — investors get their money back, then everyone shares equally in remaining proceeds. This is the best possible structure. Market standard. Guard this in Series A.
Board (2 founder / 1 investor): Founders retain board control. Excellent Seed position — most founders lose this at Series A. Use it as negotiating leverage: "We're protecting this structure."
Ownership (~20%): High end of Seed range (15–25%). After a typical Series A (20% dilution), founders could be at ~56% combined. Model your cap table through Series B before signing.
Pro-rata rights: Standard provision. Allows a16z to maintain 20% ownership in future rounds. Not harmful — signals investor confidence — but creates an obligation on your future cap table.
Negotiation leverage analysis
This is a founder-friendly term sheet overall. 1× non-participating liquidation and founder board control are the two clauses that matter most — both are in your favour. If you have a competing term sheet, use it to push pre-money from $8M to $9M. That single move is worth $200K to your team at a $50M exit. Do not negotiate on liquidation preference — you already have the best possible structure for Seed.

I like what you're building — the data moat thesis is compelling. But $10M pre-money for pre-revenue feels aggressive. We've been doing $6–7M for comparable Seed rounds. Walk me through why you need $10M valuation.
You (founder)
The $10M reflects three things: the dataset took 18 months and $400K to build — competitors can't shortcut that. We have a team that's done this before at Salesforce and OpenAI. And we have 3 enterprise contracts going live in 60 days. At $6M pre, we're selling 25% before those contracts prove what we already know.
Claude coaching
Strong anchor. You referenced the dataset moat, team credibility, and near-term traction. Now let him respond before conceding anything — silence is a power move here.
Marc Andreessen
Fair on the dataset. What if we do $8M pre with a milestone: if those 3 contracts sign within 90 days, we ratchet to $10M automatically? That protects both sides.
Live coaching hint
The ratchet offer is actually good — but counter-propose a shorter milestone window. Say: "I like the structure. Make it 60 days — we have line-of-sight on all three contracts by then — and I'll accept $8M pre with the ratchet to $10M." This shows confidence, gets you the better valuation, and closes the round faster for both parties. Never be the first to accept a counter without modifying at least one term.
